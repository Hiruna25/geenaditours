Day 02: Negombo / Kandy

After breakfast visit Kandy, en route visiting:

  • Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage
  • Herbal and Spice Garden in Mawanella

About Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage:

Established in 1975 by Sri Lanka Department of Wildlife Conservation. It was originally established to provide protection to the orphaned wild elephants found in forests of Sri Lanka. It is an orphanage and nursery for elephants. Presently more than 70 elephants live here including baby elephants. The area is about 10 hectares. Bathing and viewing are on the Maha Oya River opposite the orphanage. Bottled milk feeding is done two times per day for the baby elephants, and visitors can take part in bottle feeding.

Day 04: Kandy / Nuwara Eliya

After breakfast proceed to Nuwara Eliya.

Tea Plantations:

Tea was first planted in Sri Lanka in 1824 at the Botanical Gardens at Peradeniya, when a few plants were brought from China. More were introduced from Assam in 1839. In 1867, a Scottish Planter named James Taylor planted tea seedlings in 8 hectares of forestland in the Lookondura Estate. In 1873, the tea export industry of Sri Lanka began with a modest 23 lbs. being shipped to London.

Tea is classified by elevation into low grown, medium grown and high grown into 3 main groups. Today more than 200,000 hectares in highlands and other areas are under tea cultivation, with around 300 million kilograms exported annually (25% of world tea exports).

Nuwara Eliya:

The 'Little England' of Sri Lanka, set against beautiful backdrops of mountains, valleys, waterfalls and tea plantations. It is one of the coolest places in the island, with temperatures dropping at night. All around Nuwara Eliya you'll see evidence of British influence - houses like country cottages or Queen Ann style mansions.

Victoria Park, in the middle of town, is lovely for strolls or picnics and good for birding with some rare species. The flowers bloom in spring (March-May) and fall (August-September), when low-country folk flock to Nuwara Eliya to escape the sea level heat and humidity.

En route visits:

Day 05: Nuwara Eliya / Bentota

After breakfast proceed to Bentota.

Bentota is situated on the Northern limit of the Southern Province at the confluence of the sea at the Bentota river, 62 km from Colombo. Its location in the sun-drenched southern coast at the mouth of Bentota River and its picturesque, sandy, palm-fringed beaches which border the warm, sparkling waters of the Indian Ocean made it a natural choice for development as Sri Lanka's first planned tourist resort.

En route visits:
