Detailed Itinerary

Day 01: Airport / Negombo

Warm welcome at Bandaranayke International Airport, with refreshments travel to Negombo.

Negombo is situated by the shores of a lagoon and is an ideal place for those who want quick access to and from the airport.

Attractions in the city:

  • The old Dutch fort gate built in 1672 (now part of the prison)
  • The Dutch Canal which was then a supply route to the Dutch administration
  • Huge fish market
  • Fishing villages

Overnight stay in Negambo

Day 02: Negombo / Anuradhapura

After breakfast proceed to Anuradhapura.

Anuradhapura was the first Capital of Sri Lanka established in the 4th century BC. It was the Royal capital for 119 successive Singhalese Kings and lasted for about 1500 years. Many historic monuments and buildings still remain in this sacred land.

This city, located around 205 KM North of Colombo, is one of eight World Heritage Sites in Sri Lanka and one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the World.

En route visit:

  • Munneswaram Temple

Overnight stay in Anuradhapura

Day 03: Trincomalee / Sigiriya

After breakfast proceed to visit Sigiriya Fortress.

Sigiriya is an archaeological site in North Central Sri Lanka containing the ruins of an ancient palace complex and one of the seven world heritage sites in Sri Lanka.

Visitors can have optional Ayurveda Spa Treatment here.

Overnight stay in Sigiriya

Day 04: Sigiriya / Polonnaruwa

After breakfast proceed to Polonnaruwa.

Polonnaruwa, the 2nd most ancient Kingdom of Sri Lanka, is an interesting mix of Buddhist and Hindu structures that testify to the different Kings who reigned over this ancient Kingdom.

Polonnaruwa rose to prominence between the 11th and 13th centuries and thrived during the reign of the famous monarch, King Parakramabahu I, who created a resplendent city. His greatest achievement was the construction of the Sea of Parakrama, a huge water reservoir spanning 6280 acres.

After completing Polonnaruwa city tour, proceed to Minneriya National Park.

Minneriya National Park:

This 8889-hectare park is famous for its large population of elephants in herds of 100 to 150 roaming in the jungle and seen in the catchment area of the lake. About 300 wild elephants are recorded from the Minneriya National Park.

Overnight stay in Sigiriya

Day 05: Sigiriya / Kandy

After breakfast proceed to Kandy.

Visits:

  • Temple of Tooth - Houses Lord Buddha's sacred tooth relic and is home of the Esala Perahera festival
  • Peradeniya Botanical Gardens - Enjoy the beautiful scenes
  • Herbal and Spice garden
  • Matele Buddhist Temple
  • Dambulla Cave Temple

Kandy was the last Sinhala Kingdom until it was captured and ruled by the British in 1815.

If you intend shopping, Kandy is one of the best places for buying gems, handicrafts, silk etc.

Evening cultural dance show.

Overnight stay in Kandy

Day 06: Kandy / Nuwara Eliya

After breakfast proceed to Nuwara Eliya.

Tea Plantations:

Tea was first planted in Sri Lanka in 1824 at the Botanical Gardens at Peradeniya, when a few plants were brought from China. More were introduced from Assam in 1839. In 1867, Scottish Planter James Taylor planted tea seedlings in 8 hectares of forestland in the Lookondura Estate. In 1873, Sri Lanka's tea export industry began with 23 lbs. shipped to London.

Tea is classified by elevation into low, medium and high grown. Today over 200,000 hectares are under tea cultivation, with around 300 million kilograms exported annually (25% of world tea exports).

Nuwara Eliya:

The 'Little England' of Sri Lanka, set against beautiful backdrops of mountains, valleys, waterfalls and tea plantations. It's one of the coolest places in the island, with temperatures dropping at night. You'll see evidence of British influence in the country cottages and Queen Ann style mansions.

Victoria Park is lovely for strolls or picnics and good for birding with rare species. Flowers bloom in spring (March-May) and fall (August-September), when low-country folk flock here to escape the heat and humidity.

Overnight stay in Nuwara Eliya

Day 07: Nuwara Eliya / Bentota

After breakfast proceed to Ella.

In the Southern region lies the Galle district. Balpitiya is a little town in the district that would be quite unimportant except for the location of the Madu River, very rich in biodiversity. It passes through the wet zone of Sri Lanka opening into the large Madu Lagoon on its way to the Indian Ocean.

Overnight stay in Bentota

Day 08: Bentota / Colombo

After breakfast proceed to Colombo.

City Tour:

  • Galle Face Green
  • Old Parliament Building
  • Gangaramaya Temple
  • Independence Square
  • Viharamahadevi Park

Later continue with shopping in Colombo.

Overnight in Colombo

Day 09: Colombo / Airport

After breakfast check out and proceed to the Airport for your departure flight.

Have a Safe Flight!
